You are about to make the worst decision of your natural born life. It all starts with laziness. First you dont attend your classes. You start staying in your room and letting assignments go unfinished. Grades begin to slip and your parents begin to take notice. They threaten to kick you out of your house if you don't get it together. Professors and TAs frantically email you. You are on the brink of failing all of your courses. But none of this permeates your mind. Everyday that you continue to skip, you are more and more numb and indifferent to the situation.
You have been dropped from all your courses. Your grades were beyond saving. You have been strongly encouraged to withdraw from your university. You have decided to drop out. Your parents stick to their word. They give you until the end of the week to get out of their house. No room for a deadbeat when there are your little siblings to raise. When you get back to your house Friday night all of your belongings are boxed up and sitting by the front door. Your parents don't help you load anything into your beat up old car. Off you go into the night, no idea where you are going to go.
You end up sleeping in your car in a parking lot. You're terrified. You aren't able to determine if the people knocking on your window in the dark are in your dreams or if they're truly there. Your mind is so stressed and exhausted it can't differentiate between unconscious dreams and reality. If the people were real they definitely had something they wanted me to see. Their faces were indiscernible in the dark, but you could feel malice radiating from them. You vehemently ignored and disregarded the knocking while you were experiencing it, but once the sun rises you feel the need to seek them out later that night. They must have something for me, or at least something to say to make this entire situation okay. Entertaining these delusions was the worst choice you could have possibly made.
The mission of the day was to keep yourself from sleeping that night, so you could meet these mystery figures. You scrounge loose change from within your car, stuck between the seats, scattered across the dirty floor and manage to buy a cup of gas station coffee in an attempt to keep awake. The length of the day felt intolerable to you, dying to know what was in store for you. Finally nightfall arrives and you sit in your car anxiously awaiting whoever would make an appearance. The small digital clock on the dashboard read 3:14 a.m. You start to think this figure may not return. Your eyes grow heavier and start to flutter closed. As soon as you think you are about to surrender to the exhaustion, the knock comes. The sound startles you awake and through the glass you see the dark figure. They gesture for you to get out of the car. You exit, not entirely sure why you're trusting this stranger, but you do. They're wearing a large black hooded sweater and their hands are in the central pocket. You begin to open your mouth to ask what they want, but they beat you to it. They pull out a baggie containing a powder, and you realize this is just a drug dealer. You consider your dwindling funds and debate whether or not you needed this. You consider your mental anguish and the turmoil of your life and decide there is not much left to lose. You hand over crumpled bills from your pocket and shakily accept the baggie. You have no idea what you have just done. You have no way of forseeing the person that you are going to become just to be able to use.
Your face is sunken in. You haven't eaten in days. Not that you could afford that unnecessary expense. You've sold your car, you've begun to steal from stores and pickpocket whenever possible. You are so desperate to feed your habit. It's as if there is a cavernous maw within your mind that craves it constantly. It does not let up and it will not leave you alone until you use but even then, it is never quite enough. You say silent prayers to yourself everyday that your dealer makes their weekly appearance so you can simply make it through. You know this is no way to live but you don't feel like you know how to do anything else at this point. You have become so accustomed to hiding under bridges and within condemned buildings that you don't know how else to conduct yourself. You have been consumed by this way of life. You often try and think back to the decisions you made that have led you to this point. You wonder what would be different if you had applied yourself. You feel as though it is much too late to turn things around so you allow yourself to delve even further into this hellscape that has now become your life.
Your feet are pounding the pavement so hard you feel like your fragile body is on the verge of collapse. You hear yelling behind you commanding you to stop and lie down. You refuse and keep running. Survival mode has kicked in and your body is doing all it can to keep itself going. You knew your real fear was being forced to detox in a jail cell and your body would not let that become a reality. Just when you think you have made it out, you are tackled to the ground. Your vision goes fuzzy around the edges until everything is black. When you come to you are in the back of a car. Your vision is still fuzzy but you can recognize that it is a cop car. Your heart sinks and your entrails go cold. You want to argue and plead and beg just so you have another chance to use again. You know they will take no pity on you. You go through hours of intake processing in jail all while withdrawal symptoms quickly start to settle in under your skin. When you're finally within the cell you're beginning to sweat profusely and your digestive system feels as though it wants to drop straight out of your body. Your heart has begun to palpitate wildly. Your body is in panic mode and it wants nothing more than relief but you have no way to alleviate this pain. A guard comes and looks into your cell as you are on the floor writhing. You are struggling to breathe and your vision is starting to fade. You need it. Your body is starting to die without it. Your breathing has all but halted. Trippy glorious shapes fill your mind behind your eyes and you feel your muscles begin to relax. You manage to gasp out your final thoughts which fall on totally unsympathetic ears. "What If."
